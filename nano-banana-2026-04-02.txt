**CAPTION:**
What my Tamil grandmother knew about slow mornings that science now proves 🌅

My Paati never rushed. Even with 6 children, she'd wake early, have her filter coffee in peace, and move through mornings like a gentle river rather than a rushing waterfall.

I used to think this was just "old-fashioned" until I discovered the science behind slow mornings:

✨ Cortisol regulation: Rushing triggers our stress hormone first thing, setting us up for an anxious day (Journal of Clinical Endocrinology, 2023)
✨ Nervous system reset: Gentle mornings activate our parasympathetic response - our "rest and digest" mode
✨ Better decision-making: When we're not reactive, we make calmer choices throughout the day

My 5-minute slow morning ritual:
🍌 Wake 10 minutes earlier than needed
🍌 Three deep belly breaths before getting up
🍌 One mindful activity (my cuppa, checking on plants, or just listening to morning sounds)

The NHS recommends establishing consistent morning routines for better mental wellbeing, and honestly? It works.

Some days are still chaos (we're human!), but starting slowly when possible has transformed our family mornings.

**Always consult your GP if you're experiencing persistent stress or anxiety that affects daily life.*
